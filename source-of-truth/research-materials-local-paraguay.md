# Researchable Materials (Local / Paraguay Context)

This note lists **categories of sources and materials** you can research, request, or verify **inside Paraguay** to ground the business plan, compliance posture, and market sizing. It is a working checklist—not an endorsement of any provider. Update URLs and contact points as institutions change.

---

## 1. Migration, identity, and foreign-resident rules

| What to research | Where / how (typical) |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| Residency categories, requirements, timelines, fees | **Dirección General de Migraciones** (official communications, resolutions, forms) |
| Cedula / documentación for foreigners | Same; also consular pages if applying from abroad |
| Policy changes (press, decrees) | **Gaceta Oficial**, migration authority announcements |

**Why it matters:** Your core offer (residency + ID) must track current rules, not anecdotes.

---

## 2. Business formation, tax, and accounting

| What to research | Where / how (typical) |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| Company types (EAS, SA, etc.), registration steps | **Ministerio de Justicia** / registries as applicable; **IPS** employer obligations |
| Tax regime (IVA, IRP, renta, obligations by activity) | **Subsecretaría de Estado de Tributación (SET)**—normas, guías, calendario |
| Accounting and invoicing rules | SET + **Colegio de Contadores** (professional standards, CPE) |
| Municipal obligations (patentes, tasas) | **Municipalidad de Asunción** (or target city) |

**Why it matters:** Packages that include empresa + impuestos + contabilidad must match real compliance, not marketing copy.

---

## 3. Banking and financial system (major bottleneck)

| What to research | Where / how (typical) |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| KYC/AML expectations for non-residents and new residents | **Banco Central del Paraguay (BCP)** circulars and communications to supervised entities |
| Sector reports | BCP statistics, **financial inclusion** and system stability publications |
| Bank-specific onboarding (high level) | Public requirements from major banks (websites, sucursal policies—confirm in person) |

**Why it matters:** Your operational advantage (relationships + pre-validation) should align with what banks are actually allowed to do.

---

## 4. Real estate and investment (client use cases)

| What to research | Where / how (typical) |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| Property registration, notary chain, costs | **Registro Público**, **Colegio de Escribanos**, local notaries |
| Market prices and trends (directional) | Real estate portals, **Cámara Inmobiliaria Paraguaya** (if membership/access), broker networks |
| Rural vs urban norms | Municipal and environmental rules where land is involved |

**Why it matters:** Investor clients ($200k–$600k+) need realistic timelines and fee structures.

---

## 5. Economic and demographic statistics (justification & slides)

| What to research | Where / how (typical) |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| GDP, inflation, employment | **BCP**, **INE / DGEEC** |
| Foreign direct investment, balance of payments | **BCP**, **MIC** (investment promotion) |
| Migration statistics (where published) | Migraciones + INE where cross-published |

**Why it matters:** Economic justification for investors and partners should cite official series where possible.

---

## 6. Legal profession and ethics

| What to research | Where / how (typical) |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| Scope of practice, advertising rules | **Colegio de Abogados del Paraguay** |
| Anti-money laundering obligations for obliged subjects | **SEPRELAD** (if applicable to your structure—verify with counsel) |

**Why it matters:** Integrated “relocation system” positioning must not overstep regulated activities.

---

## 7. Local field research (non-digital but essential)

- **Structured interviews:** accountants, bankers (within compliance), real estate lawyers, migration facilitators (competitors/peers).
- **Mystery shopping:** timing and friction of key steps without introductions (to benchmark your differentiated speed).
- **Process mapping:** actual document lists and branch behaviors (often differ from websites).

---

## 8. International / European side (complement local work)

Not “local,” but usually researched in parallel: tax residency rules in clients’ home countries, CRS/reporting, EU exit formalities. Use **primary sources** (tax authority publications) and qualified advisors in each jurisdiction.

---

## Suggested outputs from this research

1. A **one-page compliance map** (migration → ID → bank → empresa → SET).  
2. A **benchmark table** (median timelines with/without facilitation—measured, not guessed).  
3. A **citation list** for the business plan (official PDFs, dates, resolution numbers).
